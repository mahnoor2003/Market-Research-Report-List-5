<p><p>An enteral feeding tube is a medical device used to deliver liquid nutrition directly into the gastrointestinal tract of patients who are unable to eat by mouth. <p><p>Enteral feeding tubes are classified into three main types in the market: Gastrostomy Tube, Nasoenteric Tube, and Other. Gastrostomy tubes are inserted directly into the stomach through the abdominal wall, providing a long-term feeding solution for patients unable to eat orally. Nasoenteric tubes, on the other hand, are inserted through the nose and into the stomach or small intestine, commonly used for short-term feeding. The Other category includes a variety of specialized enteral feeding tubes designed for specific patient needs and conditions.</p></p>
