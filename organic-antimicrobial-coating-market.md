<p><p>Organic antimicrobial coatings can be classified into two main types: low molecular and macromolecule coatings. Low molecular coatings typically consist of small organic compounds that are effective at inhibiting the growth of bacteria and other microorganisms on surfaces. In contrast, macromolecule coatings are larger molecules that form a protective barrier on surfaces, preventing the growth of microbes. Both types of coatings are commonly used in various industries to enhance the antimicrobial properties of products and surfaces.</p></p>
