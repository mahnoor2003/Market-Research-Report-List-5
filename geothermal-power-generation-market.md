Back Pressure systems make use of steam at moderate pressures for power generation. Binary systems use lower temperature fluids to generate electricity. Double Flash systems separate the geothermal water into steam and liquid for increased efficiency. Dry Steam systems utilize the steam directly from underground reservoirs. Single Flash systems separate and utilize steam for power generation. Triple Flash systems further separate the steam to increase efficiency.</p></p>
