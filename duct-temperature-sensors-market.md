<p><p>Duct temperature sensors are commonly categorized into two main types based on their working principle: Resistance Sensor and Thermocouple Sensor. Resistance sensors measure temperature by changes in electrical resistance of the sensing element, while thermocouple sensors work by generating a voltage proportional to the temperature difference between two different metals. Both types of sensors are widely used in HVAC systems and industrial applications for monitoring and controlling duct temperatures, ensuring optimal performance and efficiency.</p></p>
