<p><p>Agricultural tractors are classified into two main types based on their drive systems: Two-Wheel Drive (2WD) and Four-Wheel Drive (4WD). Two-Wheel Drive tractors are typically lighter, more maneuverable, and more fuel-efficient, making them suitable for tasks on flat and smooth terrain. On the other hand, Four-Wheel Drive tractors provide more traction and power, making them better suited for challenging terrains and heavy-duty tasks. Farmers choose between these types based on the specific needs of their land and the type of work they need to perform.</p></p>
