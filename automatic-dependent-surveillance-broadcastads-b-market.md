<p><p>Automatic Dependent Surveillance Broadcast (ADS-B) is a surveillance technology used in aviation, which allows aircraft to determine their position via satellite navigation and periodically broadcast it. There are three types of ADS-B markets: ADS-B Out, where aircraft transmit their own position; ADS-B In, where aircraft receive information from other aircraft; and ADS-B Ground Stations, which receive signals from aircraft and relay them to air traffic control. Each market plays a crucial role in enhancing the efficiency and safety of air traffic management.</p></p>
