<p><p>The Fire Alarms market consists of two main types: Addressable Systems and Conventional Systems. Addressable systems have individual devices with unique addresses that communicate with a central control panel, providing specific information about the location of a fire. Conventional systems divide a building into zones, with multiple devices connected to each zone for general detection. Addressable systems are more advanced, allowing for precise fire detection and easier maintenance, while Conventional systems are simpler and more cost-effective for smaller buildings.</p></p>
