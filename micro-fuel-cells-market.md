<p><p>Micro fuel cells are small devices that convert chemical energy into electricity through an electrochemical process. There are two main types of micro fuel cells in the market: air-cooled and liquid-cooled. Air-cooled micro fuel cells use air as the oxidizer, which eliminates the need for complex cooling systems, making them lightweight and portable. On the other hand, liquid-cooled micro fuel cells use a liquid coolant to regulate temperature and improve efficiency, making them more suitable for high-power applications that require continuous operation.</p></p>
