<p><p>Ankle prostheses can be categorized into two main types: cementless and cemented. Cementless ankle prostheses are designed to be press-fit into the bone without the use of bone cement, allowing for more natural bone growth and stability. On the other hand, cemented ankle prostheses are secured to the bone using bone cement, providing immediate stability and fixation. Both types of ankle prostheses have their own advantages and considerations, and the choice between them depends on the patient's specific needs and conditions.</p></p>
